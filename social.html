Every HR director I speak to in the UAE says the same thing in April 2026: "We don't have a talent shortage. We have a retention problem." Three things we consistently see working across our clients in construction, logistics and facility management: 1. We stopped rewarding speed-to-hire and started rewarding 12-month retention. 2. We rebuilt onboarding so workers meet their direct supervisor on Day 1 — not Day 14. 3. We audited accommodation standards annually, not reactively. None of this is revolutionary. It's just ethical. And it compounds. If your workforce is walking out the back door faster than it walks in the front, the answer isn't another agency. It's a partner who treats retention as a compliance metric — not a lucky outcome. #EthicalRecruitment #WorkforceSolutions #UAEHR #ESG #Tarkash

ESG reporting for UAE employers is no longer a "nice-to-have" conversation. Regulators want data. Investors want evidence. And your workforce is on the line. Where most organisations fall short isn't in intention — it's in the HR function's ability to generate defensible numbers: → Pay-equity audits with actual bands, not averages → DEI representation by job level, not headcount → Retention split by nationality, gender, contract type → Grievance resolution times with SLA tracking → Accommodation & human-rights due diligence on labour supply chains At Tarkash FZE, this is the HR-analytics stack we build with clients — because "we're working on it" is not an ESG disclosure. #ESG #HRAnalytics #PayEquity #DEI #SustainableHR #UAEBusiness

Thirty years in the UAE labour market has taught me one thing: You cannot outsource dignity. You can outsource sourcing. Documentation. Mobilisation. Training. Payroll. But the moment you outsource how a worker is treated — at the camp, at the gate, in the queue, on the call — you have outsourced your brand. The best companies I've worked with in this region don't audit their agencies annually. They audit them continuously — and they treat the labour supply chain with the same seriousness as their financial supply chain. Responsible recruitment is not complicated. It is just committed. If you'd like to talk about what that looks like in practice — my inbox is open. #EthicalRecruitment #Leadership #WorkerWelfare #UAE #HRTransformation

Mini case study: A logistics client needed 60 workers mobilised for a port expansion in 72 hours. The brief was simple. The constraints were not: • Mixed roles: forklift, loaders, supervisors • Accommodation ready before arrival • Full UAE compliance on documentation • Zero repatriation in first six months We delivered in 68 hours. Retention at month six is 98%. How? Three things: 1. A pre-vetted, documented talent pool we invest in before the client calls — not after. 2. Dedicated deployment coordinators per project, not per agency desk. 3. Day-1 supervisor match, not "we'll sort it on site." On-demand labour supply doesn't have to compromise quality. It just has to be designed for it. #LabourSupply #Logistics #UAEWorkforce #Recruitment #SupplyChain

Emiratisation is not a quota. It's a leadership decision. Nafis targets will get you compliance. They will not get you a UAE national who stays for a decade, mentors three more, and becomes the reason your organisation matters locally. What actually works — from what we've seen across construction, retail and facility-management clients: → Pair every Emirati hire with a named senior mentor on Day 1 → Guarantee a 24-month career-path conversation in the contract → Invest in language, leadership and technical upskilling — fund it, don't bill it → Measure internal-mobility rate for UAE nationals as a board KPI → Celebrate the promotions publicly. Quietly celebrate the resignations you prevented. Emiratisation done well is a loyalty engine. Emiratisation done for compliance is a rotating door. Which one is yours running? #Emiratisation #Nafis #UAE #LeadershipDevelopment #Talent #DEI
